The Biology Behind ALS

Under cellular stress, neurons form stress granules to protect RNA. In ALS, persistent granules seed pathological TDP-43 aggregation, accelerating neuronal degeneration.

Cellular mechanism illustration

Our Dual-Layered Solution

Granulyze combines antisense oligonucleotides to reduce stress granule formation with RNA aptamers that inhibit toxic TDP-43 aggregation.
